## About This Essay

This essay introduces cognitive amplification - a methodology for human-AI collaboration that is not about replacing human thinking but about expressing it at a scale and precision that would be impossible to sustain alone. It documents how one researcher used a two-layer AI architecture (advisory AI and implementer AI, with human directorial control at every decision point) to produce a rigorous empirical research program over 18 months without institutional backing.

The essay grounds its argument in two case studies drawn from lived experience: a research taxonomy project (the YIM Core Six AI Defensive Behavior Framework) and a real-time legal crisis (housing habitability dispute, no attorney, active flooding). The primary research corpus referenced throughout is:

> Taylor, E. A. (2026). *From Micro-Failure Tags to Defensive Syndromes: A Technical Framework for the Core Six User-Facing Failure Modes in AI Assistants.* DOI: [10.5281/zenodo.19423182](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19423182)
